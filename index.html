Lip Reading System using Deep Learning (FYP)

Our project aims to develop a lip-reading system that captures and analyzes lip motion patterns to predict spoken words, enhancing communication for mute and deaf individuals.

  • 𝗢𝗯𝗷𝗲𝗰𝘁𝗶𝘃𝗲𝘀:

    • Our main goal was to create a reliable and accurate lip reading model that interprets lip movements to predict words, bridging the communication gap for those with speech and hearing impairments.

  • 𝗠𝗲𝘁𝗵𝗼𝗱𝗼𝗹𝗼𝗴𝘆:
  • 𝗔𝗽𝗽𝗿𝗼𝗮𝗰𝗵 𝟭:

    • We used the Inception V3 model with a dataset of videos from 3 males and 3 females. Initially, we struggled due to processing limitations. By applying data augmentation and expanding our dataset to 15,000 videos, we improved our validation accuracy to over 85%.

  • 𝗔𝗽𝗽𝗿𝗼𝗮𝗰𝗵 𝟮:

    • We designed a mask with SONAR sensors to capture lip motion patterns. The mask, using Arduino UNO, created a dataset for 10 words, which trained a Random Forest model. This approach showed promising results in accurately predicting words and we improved our validation accuracy to over 80%.

  • 𝗥𝗲𝘀𝘂𝗹𝘁𝘀:

    • Our project achieved significant improvements in prediction accuracy and system reliability, demonstrating the feasibility of using machine learning and sensor technologies to enhance communication for mute and deaf individuals.

  • 𝗖𝗼𝗻𝗰𝗹𝘂𝘀𝗶𝗼𝗻:

    • The "Robust Lip Reading System" highlights the potential of advanced technologies to develop effective communication aids. This project lays a strong foundation for future advancements, promising greater vocabulary expansion and improved accuracy, contributing to better communication and quality of life for individuals with speech impairments.
